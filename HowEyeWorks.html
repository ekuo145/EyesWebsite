Overview

When we see, what we actually see are electromagnetic rays at the wavelength of visible light.

We see different color light rays, where when something appears a certain color, it means the object has abosrbed everything except that color.

The eye starts by taking in light using its photoreceptors, and turning it into nerve signals that the brain can understand

The photoreceptors create action potentials that go to the visual cortex.
